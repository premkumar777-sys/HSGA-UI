About HSGA Telangana

Institutional identity, history, mission, and legal standing of the Hindustan Scouts & Guides Association, Telangana State.

Who We Are

More Than a Movement — A Mission.

The Hindustan Scouts & Guides Association (HSGA), Telangana State, is the official state scouting authority — recognised by the Government of Telangana and affiliated with the Bharat Scouts and Guides (BSG) at the national level, and the World Organization of the Scout Movement (WOSM) and the World Association of Girl Guides and Girl Scouts (WAGGS) internationally.

Operating across all 33 districts of Telangana, HSGA functions as a non-profit, youth-development body dedicated to the holistic growth of children and young adults through structured scouting and guiding programs aligned with India's national education and development priorities.

Under the leadership of Dr. Kunchapu Suresh, State Secretary, HSGA Telangana administers six progressive sections serving students from age 6 to 25, equipping them with life skills, civic values, leadership abilities, and a deep sense of national identity — all through the universal language of scouting.

We are not a supplementary activity. We are a national character-building institution operating inside every school we affiliate with.

Our Journey

Seven Decades of Shaping Telangana's Youth

1907
The Global Beginning

Lord Baden-Powell founds the Scout Movement at Brownsea Island, UK. A global youth revolution begins.

1909
Scouting Reaches India

The first Scout troop in India is established. The movement spreads rapidly across the subcontinent.

1949
HSGA Founded

Hindustan Scouts & Guides Association is formally established, with Hyderabad (then Hyderabad State) as a founding state unit.

1966
BSG National Affiliation

HSGA affiliates with the newly constituted Bharat Scouts and Guides — the unified national scouting body of India.

1990s
Expansion Across Andhra Pradesh

HSGA units proliferate across schools in the combined state of Andhra Pradesh, reaching tens of thousands of students.

2014
Telangana State Formation

With the formation of Telangana as the 29th state of India, HSGA Telangana is constituted as a separate state unit — a historic milestone.

2020
NEP 2020 Integration

HSGA Telangana becomes a recognised co-curricular partner under the National Education Policy 2020, validating scouting as academic credit.

2025–26
New Era — 1,200+ Schools

Under Dr. Kunchapu Suresh, HSGA Telangana surpasses 1,200 affiliated schools and 85,000 active members — its largest ever footprint.

Cub Scouts

Boys aged 6 to 10 years
Beginner Section · Pack System · Akela Guided

Who are Cubs? Cubs are the youngest and most energetic members of the Scout family. Led by their "Akela" (Pack Leader), they learn through games, stories, crafts, and outdoor adventures — building the values that will guide them for life.

What makes Cubs special? The entire program is designed to be joyful and discovery-led. Every activity has a purpose — whether it's building friendship, learning to clean up after themselves, understanding traffic rules, or planting their first sapling.

Scouts

Boys aged 10 to 17 years
Core Section · Troop System · Scout Master Led

The Heart of Scouting. The Scout section is the core of the entire movement. Scouts undergo progressive training in outdoor skills, leadership, community service, and character development — with a structured badge system that culminates in the prestigious President Award.

Each Scout Troop (15–32 members) is divided into Patrols of 5–8 Scouts, each led by a Patrol Leader — developing peer leadership from day one. The Scout Master provides overall guidance and mentorship.

Implementation Model

How HSGA Works Inside Your School

📅
Weekly Meetings

One 45-minute meeting per week during school hours or as an after-school activity — structured by the Scout Master or Guide Captain.

🗓️
Monthly Activities

One outdoor or community service activity per month — camps, service drives, cultural events, and district-level competitions.

📊
Annual Assessment

Badge examinations, logbook reviews, and rank assessments conducted by the District Commissioner — all documented officially.

Infrastructure Requirements

What Your School Needs to Begin

👨‍🏫
Designated Teacher

Minimum one teacher willing to serve as Scout Master or Guide Captain, who will attend HSGA's 3-day certification training.

👦
Minimum Students

At least 16 student members per section to form a valid Pack, Troop, or Company. No maximum limit.

🏫
Meeting Space

A classroom, hall, or outdoor space for weekly meetings — no special facility required. Your existing school campus is sufficient.

📋
School Registration

Valid school registration certificate from the Telangana Department of School Education or equivalent authority.

💰
Annual Affiliation Fee

Affordable annual fee as per HSGA Telangana schedule. Concessions for government schools and rural/tribal institutions.

📝
Principal's Commitment

A signed undertaking from the school principal confirming support for the HSGA program within the school's academic calendar.

5-Step Enrollment Process

From Application to First Uniform — 5 Steps.

1
Download & Complete Application

Download Form HSGA/REG/001 from this website or collect it from your nearest District Commissioner's office. Complete all sections of the form with accurate school details, teacher nomination, and student count.

2
Submit to District Commissioner

Submit the completed application along with: School Registration Certificate, Principal's Letter of Intent, Teacher Nominee's details, Student Strength Statement (class-wise), and prescribed affiliation fee.

3
Inspection & Verification

A District Commissioner representative visits the school to verify details and conduct a brief orientation with the principal and nominated teacher. Formal affiliation approval is issued within 14 working days.

4
Teacher Certification Training

The nominated Scout Master or Guide Captain attends a 3-day HSGA residential certification training at the District Training Centre — equipping them with the skills to run an effective Scout or Guide unit.

5
Unit Formation & Launch

With certification in hand, the school officially launches its Scout or Guide unit. Students are enrolled, uniforms and logbooks are issued, and the first official meeting is held — marking the beginning of a transformative journey.

SDG Impact

Telangana's Scouts and Guides are active contributors to the United Nations' 2030 Agenda for Sustainable Development.

Global Responsibility

Six SDGs. Thousands of Actions. One Generation.

HSGA Telangana doesn't just talk about the United Nations Sustainable Development Goals — our students live them, through every camp, every service project, and every badge earned.

SDG 3

Good Health & Well-Being

First aid certification, health & hygiene programs, mental wellness awareness, nutrition education through cooking badge, and yoga/physical fitness in all sections.

→ 85,000+ students trained in basic health practices
SDG 4

Quality Education

HSGA complements classroom education with life skills, leadership, outdoor learning, and co-curricular development — embodying NEP 2020's vision of holistic quality education for all.

→ 1,200+ schools enriched with structured co-curricular programs
SDG 11

Sustainable Communities

Monthly Swachh Bharat drives, disaster preparedness training, community infrastructure projects, and civic awareness programs make Scouts active contributors to sustainable community building.

→ 500+ community service projects annually
SDG 13

Climate Action

Haritha Haram plantation drives, biodiversity documentation, water conservation campaigns, and eco-badge programs actively position Telangana's youth as climate change warriors.

→ 50,000 saplings planted — 2026 target
SDG 15

Life on Land

Ecology walks, wildlife awareness, habitat documentation, organic farming badge, and forest conservation activities connect Scouts to Telangana's rich natural biodiversity.

→ Active in 15 eco-sensitive zones across Telangana
SDG 16

Peace, Justice & Institutions

National integration programs, inter-faith cultural events, civic education through the Scout Law, and disaster relief volunteerism build peace-promoting, just, and inclusive communities.

When ZPHS Warangal affiliated with HSGA in 2019, they had zero co-curricular framework. By 2023, they had produced 3 Rajya Puraskar awardees, conducted 12 community service projects, and were recognised by the District Collector for their Swachh Bharat initiative.

"Our students' attendance and academic performance both improved after joining HSGA. The structure, discipline, and sense of purpose changed the school culture entirely." — Mr. Ravi Shankar, Headmaster

Pragatisheel Prashikshan Yojana

The progressive training framework that transforms curious children into certified leaders — from age 3 to 25.

The Training Blueprint

Age-Wise Progressive Learning System

HSGA's Pragatisheel Prashikshan Yojana (Progressive Training Plan) is a structured, age-appropriate curriculum that guides youth through four distinct development phases — each building on the last, culminating in India's highest scouting honour: the President Award.

🐰
Ages 3–5

Bunney

Pre-Scout Foundation
The earliest introduction to scouting values. Bunney activities focus on play-based learning, basic manners, hygiene habits, and love for nature. The seed of the Scout spirit is planted here.
Play-based Learning Good Manners Nature Love Hygiene Habits
Entry Certificate

Cubs / Bulbuls

Foundation Section
The first formal scouting section. Cubs and Bulbuls learn civic values, teamwork, and outdoor basics through structured pack activities led by the Akela (Pack Leader).
Civic Values Team Games Craft Activities Good Deeds
